Genesis: The New Standard of Luxury

Genesis is the youngest luxury brand in the world, yet it has already established itself as a serious competitor to the German establishment.

The Genesis name first appeared in 2008 as a model – the Hyundai Genesis, a rear-wheel drive luxury sedan. It was a statement of intent: Hyundai could build a credible rival to the BMW 5 Series.

The Genesis sedan was followed by the Genesis Coupe (2009), a sporty 2+2. Both proved Hyundai's engineering capability. But the real move came in 2015, when Hyundai spun off Genesis as a standalone luxury brand.

The first Genesis models were rebadged: the G80 (formerly Genesis sedan) and G90 (formerly Equus). But new models followed quickly, with distinctive design and technology.

The 2017 G70 was a compact sports sedan aimed at the BMW 3 Series. It won Motor Trend Car of the Year in 2019 – a huge achievement for a new brand.

The GV80 (2020) was Genesis's first SUV – a stunning, luxurious, and capable vehicle. The GV70 (2021) followed, becoming an instant success.

Today, Genesis offers a full lineup of sedans and SUVs, all with stunning design, luxurious interiors, and advanced technology. The brand has won numerous awards, including being ranked #1 in J.D. Power Initial Quality Study.

Genesis is now going electric, with the GV60 (2022) and Electrified G80, and plans for a full EV lineup by 2030. The brand has established itself as a genuine luxury contender.

Athletic Elegance Design

Genesis design is distinctive, bold, and instantly recognizable. Led by Luc Donckerwolke (former Bentley and Lamborghini designer) and SangYup Lee.

Key Elements:

  • Crest Grille: Large, shield-shaped grille – Genesis signature
  • Quad Lamps: Two-line headlights and taillights – distinctive
  • Parabolic Line: Flowing shoulder line
  • G-Matrix: Diamond-pattern details

Design Philosophy:

"Athletic Elegance" combines dynamic proportions with graceful lines. Genesis cars look sporty yet sophisticated – a difficult balance.

Designers:

  • Luc Donckerwolke (Chief Creative Officer)
  • SangYup Lee (Head of Design)
  • Former Bentley, Lamborghini, Chevrolet talent

The Genesis Experience

Genesis rethinks the luxury ownership experience. It's not just about the car – it's about how you're treated.

Genesis Difference:

  • Genesis Concierge: Personal assistant for all needs
  • Valet Service: They pick up your car for service, leave a loaner
  • Genesis At Home: Test drives at your home or office
  • Genesis Studio: Urban showrooms for browsing
  • Genesis House: Cultural spaces in New York, Seoul

Interior Luxury:

  • Nappa leather, real wood, metal details
  • Lexicon audio systems
  • Ergonomically designed seats
  • Minimalist, driver-focused layout

Genesis consistently ranks #1 in J.D. Power Initial Quality Study – a testament to build quality and reliability.

Electrified Future

Genesis plans to be an all-electric brand by 2030, with 8 EV models.

Electrified Lineup:

  • GV60 (2022): First dedicated EV
  • Electrified G80 (2022): Electric sedan
  • Electrified GV70 (2023): Electric SUV
  • GV90 (2024): Flagship electric SUV
  • Electrified G90 (2025): Electric flagship sedan

E-GMP Platform:

  • 800V architecture – 10-80% in 18 minutes
  • Up to 300+ mile range
  • Vehicle-to-Load (V2L) power export
  • High-performance versions

X Concept (2021) and X Speedium Coupe (2022) preview the design future – stunning electric GTs.
