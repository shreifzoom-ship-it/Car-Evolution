Jeep: The Original Off-Roader

Jeep's story begins with World War II. In 1940, the US Army needed a light reconnaissance vehicle. It invited 135 manufacturers to bid; only two responded. Willys-Overland and Ford produced prototypes, and the Willys MA (later MB) was selected.

The Willys MB, along with the Ford GPW, became the iconic "Jeep." The origin of the name is debated – some say it's from "GP" (General Purpose), others from a Popeye character "Eugene the Jeep." Whatever the origin, the Jeep became legendary for its toughness and versatility.

After the war, Willys saw civilian potential. The 1945 CJ-2A (Civilian Jeep) was the first production off-road vehicle for the public. It was basic – canvas top, no doors, simple mechanics – but capable. The CJ (Civilian Jeep) line continued through 1986.

In 1949, Willys introduced the Jeep Wagon, the first all-steel station wagon, and the Jeep Truck. These expanded Jeep beyond pure off-roaders.

The 1963 Wagoneer was revolutionary – the first luxury 4x4 with automatic transmission and independent front suspension. It was ahead of its time and is considered the first true SUV.

American Motors Corporation (AMC) bought Jeep in 1970 and modernized the lineup. The 1974 Cherokee (SJ) was a two-door version of Wagoneer, creating the sporty SUV segment. The 1984 XJ Cherokee was groundbreaking – unibody construction, lighter, more efficient, and hugely successful.

Chrysler bought AMC in 1987 primarily for Jeep. The Wrangler (YJ) replaced the CJ, with square headlights (controversial). The 1993 Grand Cherokee launched as a luxury SUV, creating the modern SUV market.

Today, Jeep is a global brand under Stellantis, with models ranging from the rugged Wrangler to the luxurious Grand Cherokee and the compact Renegade. The upcoming Wagoneer and Grand Wagoneer return to the luxury full-size segment. Jeep remains the benchmark for off-road capability.

Iconic Models

Willys MB / Ford GPW (1941-1945)

1941-1945

The original Jeep. Over 600,000 built for WWII. Lightweight, rugged, and versatile. The grandfather of all off-road vehicles.

  • 2.2L 4-cylinder, 60 hp
  • 2,400 lbs
  • WWII legend
  • Go-Devil engine

Jeep Wrangler

1987-present

The direct descendant of the original Jeep. Solid axles, removable doors and top, and legendary off-road capability. The ultimate 4x4.

Jeep Cherokee XJ

1984-2001

Revolutionary unibody SUV. Lighter, more efficient, and hugely successful. The XJ created the modern compact SUV segment and has a massive cult following.

Jeep Grand Cherokee

1993-present

The luxury SUV that defined the segment. Combined off-road capability with on-road comfort and luxury. Four generations, millions sold.

Jeep Wagoneer / Grand Wagoneer

1963-1991, 2022-present

The first luxury 4x4. Woodgrain sides, luxurious interior, and ahead of its time. Revived in 2022 as a full-size luxury SUV.

Jeep CJ-5

1954-1983

The longest-running CJ. Over 600,000 built. The definitive post-war Jeep, loved by farmers, off-roaders, and enthusiasts.

Jeep Gladiator

2020-present

The Wrangler-based pickup. Removable doors and top, off-road capability, and a truck bed. The ultimate adventure vehicle.

Jeep Compass

2007-present

The compact crossover, bringing Jeep capability to a smaller, more efficient package.

The Wrangler – King of Off-Road

The Wrangler is the direct descendant of the original Willys MB. It's the most capable production off-road vehicle in the world.

Generations:

  • YJ (1987-1995): First Wrangler. Square headlights (controversial), wider track, better on-road manners.
  • TJ (1997-2006): Return to round headlights. Coil springs (finally), better ride, Rubicon trim introduced (2003).
  • JK (2007-2018): Larger, more refined. Four-door Unlimited model introduced – game-changer. More power, more capability.
  • JL (2018-present): Current generation. Lighter, more efficient, advanced technology. Diesel, 4xe plug-in hybrid, 392 V8 options.

Rubicon:

The Rubicon trim is named after the Rubicon Trail in California, one of the toughest off-road trails. It features locking differentials, disconnecting sway bar, low-range transfer case, and heavy-duty axles. It's ready for the hardest trails straight from the factory.

4xe:

The plug-in hybrid Wrangler offers 49 MPGe, 21 miles electric range, and instant torque for off-road. The best-selling PHEV in America.

The Wrangler's removable doors and top, fold-down windshield, and solid axles keep it true to the original Jeep spirit.

Grand Cherokee – The Luxury SUV

The Grand Cherokee created the modern luxury SUV segment. It combined off-road capability with on-road comfort and luxury, a formula that every manufacturer now follows.

Generations:

  • ZJ (1993-1998): First generation. Based on Chrysler's new platform, it was a revelation. V8 option, luxurious interior, and genuine off-road ability.
  • WJ (1999-2004): Refined design, Quadra-Drive system, more luxury. Overland trim introduced.
  • WK (2005-2010): Bolder design, independent front suspension (controversial but better on-road). SRT8 version with 420 hp.
  • WK2 (2011-2021): Lighter, more efficient, available diesel. Trackhawk version with 707 hp – insane SUV.
  • WL (2021-present): Current generation. Three rows available, advanced luxury, plug-in 4xe version.

Performance Versions:

  • SRT8 (2006-2010): 6.1L HEMI, 420 hp
  • SRT8 (2012-2021): 6.4L HEMI, 475 hp
  • Trackhawk (2018-2021): Supercharged 6.2L Hellcat, 707 hp – 0-60 in 3.5 seconds

Jeep Off-Road Technology

Locking Differentials

Front and rear locking diffs allow power to go to all wheels even if some have no traction. Essential for serious off-roading.

Rock-Trac 4x4

Heavy-duty transfer case with 4:1 low-range ratio. Crawl ratio of 84:1 for precise control at low speeds.

Disconnecting Sway Bar

Electronic disconnecting sway bar allows maximum wheel articulation on rough terrain, keeping tires on the ground.

Water Fording

Wrangler can ford up to 30 inches of water. Air intake is high in the engine bay.

Approach / Departure Angles

Wrangler Rubicon: 44° approach, 37° departure, 27.8° breakover – can climb over huge obstacles.

4xe Electric Torque

Instant torque from electric motors provides precise control off-road, with regenerative braking for descents.
