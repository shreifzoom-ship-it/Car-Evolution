Kia: Movement that inspires

Kia is Korea's oldest car manufacturer, founded in 1944 as a manufacturer of bicycle parts and steel tubing. The name "Kia" means "to rise from Asia."

After WWII, Kia built bicycles, then motorcycles, then trucks. The first Kia passenger car, the Brisa, appeared in 1974. It was a small car based on Mazda technology.

Through the 1980s, Kia partnered with Ford and Mazda, building the Mazda-based Pride (Ford Festiva). Quality was basic, but prices were low.

The 1990s saw Kia expand globally, but the Asian financial crisis hit hard. Kia filed for bankruptcy in 1997 and was acquired by Hyundai in 1998, forming the Hyundai Motor Group.

Under Hyundai, Kia was allowed to operate independently but with shared platforms and R&D. The 2000s brought rapid growth. Kia hired Peter Schreyer (former Audi designer) in 2006, and his influence transformed the brand.

Schreyer's "tiger nose" grille became Kia's signature. Models like the Optima, Sportage, and Soul gained distinctive styling. Quality improved dramatically.

The 2017 Stinger was a revelation – a rear-wheel drive sports sedan that could compete with BMW and Audi. It proved Kia could build a genuine driver's car.

Today, Kia is a design-led brand with a stunning lineup. The EV6 electric crossover won multiple awards. The Telluride SUV is one of America's most acclaimed vehicles. Kia has shed its cheap image entirely.

"Design is the soul of our brand."
- Peter Schreyer, Design Chief

The Design Revolution

When Peter Schreyer joined Kia in 2006, he transformed the brand. He was the first foreign design chief in a Korean company and later became the first non-Korean president.

Schreyer's Impact:

  • Introduced "tiger nose" grille – Kia's signature
  • Clean, European-inspired lines
  • Premium materials and proportions
  • Designed the Stinger himself

Opposites United (Current):

Kia's new design philosophy, led by Karim Habib (ex-BMW). Based on contrast and harmony – combining sharp edges with organic surfaces. Seen on EV6, EV9, K5.

Design Icons:

  • Peter Schreyer (2006-2018)
  • Karim Habib (2019-present)
  • Gregory Guillaume (Stinger designer)

The Stinger – Gran Turismo

The Stinger was Kia's statement of intent. A rear-wheel drive sports sedan with a twin-turbo V6, designed to compete with the best from Europe.

Development:

Developed under Albert Biermann (ex-BMW M) and tested at the Nürburgring. The chassis was tuned for genuine driving pleasure.

Models:

  • 2.0L Turbo: 255 hp, 4-cylinder
  • 3.3L Twin-Turbo V6: 365 hp, 0-60 in 4.6s
  • GT: V6 with Brembo brakes, adaptive suspension
  • GT1/GT2: Loaded versions

Legacy:

The Stinger proved Kia could build a genuine driver's car. It won praise worldwide and became a cult favorite. Production ended in 2023, but its impact remains.

EV Lineup

Kia is committed to electrification with a dedicated EV lineup built on the E-GMP platform (shared with Hyundai and Genesis).

Current EVs:

  • EV6 (2021): Crossover, up to 328 mile range, GT with 576 hp
  • Niro EV (2022): Compact SUV, practical and efficient
  • EV9 (2023): Large three-row SUV, 350 mile range, 800V charging
  • EV5 (2024): Mid-size SUV (global markets)
  • EV4 / EV3 (2025): Compact sedans/hatches

E-GMP Advantages:

  • 800V architecture – 10-80% charge in 18 minutes
  • Vehicle-to-Load (V2L) – power external devices
  • Up to 576 hp (EV6 GT)
  • Ultra-fast charging network access
