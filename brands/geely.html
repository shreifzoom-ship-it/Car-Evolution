Geely: China's Global Automotive Powerhouse

Geely's story is one of the most remarkable in modern automotive history. Founded in 1986 by Li Shufu as a refrigerator parts manufacturer, it has grown into a global automotive empire that owns Volvo, Lotus, and stakes in Aston Martin and Mercedes-Benz [citation:1][citation:5].

Li Shufu, a visionary entrepreneur, started Geely in Taizhou, Zhejiang Province, with borrowed funds from his family. The company initially made refrigerator parts before transitioning to motorcycles in 1994, producing China's first scooter [citation:9].

In 1997, Geely made the bold move to enter the automotive industry, becoming China's first privately-owned automaker. The first Geely car, the Haoqing, rolled off the assembly line in 1998, but the company didn't receive its official production license until November 2001 [citation:1][citation:9].

The 2000s saw rapid expansion. Geely listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange in 2005, becoming the first Chinese automaker to do so. The company also began exporting cars and established research and development centers to improve quality and technology [citation:5].

In 2010, Geely shocked the automotive world by acquiring Volvo Cars from Ford Motor Company for $1.8 billion. This was the largest overseas acquisition by a Chinese automaker at the time. Under Geely's ownership, Volvo has thrived, with sales reaching record levels and new platforms developed [citation:9].

Geely continued its acquisition spree: acquiring the London Taxi Company (now LEVC) in 2013, Lotus and Proton in 2017, and stakes in Daimler AG (Mercedes-Benz) and Aston Martin. The company also launched new brands like Lynk & Co and Zeekr [citation:5][citation:9].

Today, Geely Holding Group ranks among the Fortune Global 500 for 13 consecutive years, with cumulative global sales exceeding 17 million vehicles. The company operates R&D centers in China, Sweden, and the UK, and is a leader in electric vehicle technology with its SEA (Sustainable Experience Architecture) platform [citation:1][citation:5].

In 2025, Geely officially launched its own brand in the UK market with the EX5 electric SUV, marking another milestone in its global expansion [citation:5].

Iconic Models

Geely Haoqing (1998-2004)

1998-2004

The first Geely car. Based on a Daihatsu platform, this small hatchback started Geely's journey in the automotive industry. Simple, affordable, and essential for China's emerging middle class [citation:5].

  • 1.0L-1.3L engines
  • First private Chinese car
  • Launched 1998
  • Affordable mobility

Geely Emgrand EC7

2009-2016

Geely's breakthrough model. The EC7 proved that Chinese cars could be well-built, safe (Euro NCAP 4-star), and stylish. It became Geely's best-seller and export champion [citation:1].

Geely Borui (GC9)

2015-present

Geely's flagship sedan, designed by former Volvo designer Peter Horbury. It marked Geely's entry into the mid-size segment with premium aspirations [citation:1].

Geely Boyue (Proton X70)

2016-present

Popular SUV that also became the Proton X70 in Malaysia. Stylish, well-equipped, and successful in multiple markets [citation:9].

Geely Geometry A

2019-present

Geely's first dedicated electric model under the Geometry sub-brand. 500 km range, minimalist design, and advanced tech [citation:1].

Geely EX5

2025-present

Geely's first model for the UK market. Electric SUV targeting Tesla Model Y, priced under £40,000. Marks Geely's direct entry into Europe [citation:5].

Lynk & Co 01

2017-present

First model from Geely's innovative Lynk & Co brand. CMA platform, subscription-based ownership model, and distinctive design [citation:9].

Zeekr 001

2021-present

Geely's premium electric shooting brake. SEA platform, 0-100 in 3.8s, and 700 km range. Sold in Europe and China [citation:9].

The Global Empire

Geely Holding Group is one of the most successful Chinese automotive conglomerates, with an extraordinary portfolio of brands and partnerships [citation:1][citation:9].

Major Acquisitions:

  • 2010: Volvo Cars (100%) – acquired from Ford for $1.8B
  • 2013: London Taxi Company (LEVC) – makers of London black cabs
  • 2017: Lotus (51%) – British sports car legend
  • 2017: Proton (49.9%) – Malaysian national automaker
  • 2017: Terrafugia – American flying car company
  • 2018: Daimler AG (9.69%) – Mercedes-Benz parent
  • 2022: Aston Martin (17%) – British luxury brand

Strategic Partnerships:

  • Renault: Joint venture for powertrain technology (Horse Powertrain)
  • Mercedes-Benz: Smart joint venture for electric vehicles
  • Baidu: Collaboration on autonomous driving
  • Foxconn: Contract manufacturing agreement

Geely's global reach extends to over 80 countries, with manufacturing plants across China and assembly operations worldwide. The company has been on the Fortune Global 500 list for 13 consecutive years [citation:1].

Technology Leadership

SEA Platform

Sustainable Experience Architecture. Open-source EV platform with the world's largest bandwidth (A to E segments). Developed with over 18 billion RMB investment. Cuts development time by 50% [citation:1].

CMA Architecture

Compact Modular Architecture developed with Volvo at CEVT in Sweden. Used by Volvo, Geely, Lynk & Co, and Proton. 609 patents, developed by 2,000+ engineers from 25 countries [citation:1].

GEA Architecture

Global Intelligent New Energy Architecture. World's first 4-in-1 AI architecture combining hardware, system, ecology, and AI. Supports pure electric, hybrid, range extender, and green methanol [citation:1].

Short Blade Battery

Lithium-iron phosphate (LFP) battery technology with improved safety and energy density. Passes six stringent tests including nail penetration and extreme temperatures [citation:5].

Satellite Network

Geely's "Future Mobility Constellation" – 41 of 72 initial satellites already in orbit (as of 2025). Provides precise positioning and connectivity for autonomous vehicles [citation:5].

5G & Black Light Factories

Fully automated factories producing one car every 2 minutes. "Black light" factories operate without manual intervention, using robots and cloud services [citation:1].
