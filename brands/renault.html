Renault: French Innovation Since 1899

Renault was founded in 1899 by Louis Renault and his brothers Marcel and Fernand. Louis was a brilliant engineer who invented the first direct-drive gearbox (a revolutionary improvement).

The first Renault, the Type A, was a small car with a De Dion engine. By 1903, Renault was building its own engines. The company grew rapidly, selling taxis and commercial vehicles.

During WWI, Renault built the FT-17 tank, one of the most influential tank designs in history. After the war, Renault became France's largest automaker.

Between the wars, Renault produced a range of cars from small (the Celtaquatre) to luxurious (the Reinastella). Louis Renault was a brilliant but difficult man.

After WWII, Louis Renault was accused of collaboration and died in prison under controversial circumstances. The company was nationalized and became Régie Nationale des Usines Renault.

The 4CV (1946) was the first French car to sell over a million units. The Dauphine (1956) was a global success, selling in the US and beyond.

The 1960s brought the Renault 4 (1961) – a practical, quirky, and hugely successful small car. The Renault 8 and 16 (1965) introduced the hatchback concept (Car of the Year 1966).

The 1970s and 1980s were Renault's golden age of innovation. The Renault 5 (1972) was a stylish supermini. The Renault 5 Turbo was a mid-engine rally monster. The Espace (1984) created the MPV segment. The Renault 9 (1981) won Car of the Year.

Renault partnered with Volvo in the 1990s, then acquired a controlling stake in Nissan in 1999, forming the Renault-Nissan Alliance (now Renault-Nissan-Mitsubishi).

Today, Renault produces a full range of cars from the Twingo to the Mégane, Scénic, and Captur. The brand is a leader in electric vehicles with the Zoe and the new Mégane E-Tech. Alpine has been revived as a sports car brand with the A110.

Iconic Models

Renault 5 (1972-1996) & 5 Turbo

1972-1996

The stylish supermini that defined an era. The 5 Turbo was a mid-engine, turbocharged rally monster with 160 hp in a tiny body.

  • 0.8L to 1.4L engines (standard)
  • 5 Turbo: 160 hp, 0-60 in 6.5s
  • 5.5 million sold
  • Michel Boué design

Renault Clio

1990-present

Best-selling French car ever. Clio I (1990), II (1998), III (2005), IV (2012), V (2019). Clio Williams and Clio RS are hot hatch legends.

Renault Espace

1984-present

The first MPV. Created a new category – the people carrier. Five generations, still in production.

Renault 4

1961-1992

The French Beetle. Quirky, practical, and hugely successful. Over 8 million sold in 31 years.

Renault 16

1965-1980

1966 Car of the Year. First hatchback. Innovative and spacious.

Renault 4CV

1946-1961

First French car to sell 1M+. Rear-engine, small, and economical.

Renault Mégane

Family hatchback. Mégane RS is a hot hatch icon with front-wheel drive lap records.

Renault Zoe

2012-present

Europe's best-selling electric car. Practical, efficient, and affordable.

The Clio – French Best-Seller

The Clio is Renault's most successful model. Over 15 million sold since 1990.

Generations:

  • Clio I (1990-1998): Giugiaro design, Car of the Year 1991
  • Clio II (1998-2005): Rounded design, huge success
  • Clio III (2005-2012): Larger, Car of the Year 2006
  • Clio IV (2012-2019): Sporty design, turbo engines
  • Clio V (2019-present): Modern tech, hybrid option

Hot Clios:

  • Clio Williams (1993): Limited edition, 2.0L, gold wheels
  • Clio RS (1998-2018): 2.0L naturally aspirated then 1.6L turbo
  • Clio V6 (2000-2005): Mid-engine, 230 hp, insane

The Clio is the heart of Renault – stylish, practical, and with genuine driving fun in RS versions.

Espace – Inventor of the MPV

The Espace created the MPV (Multi-Purpose Vehicle) segment. It was a radical idea – a spacious, flexible people carrier based on a van platform.

Generations:

  • Espace I (1984-1991): Matra-built, innovative, Car of the Year
  • Espace II (1991-1997): More rounded, still Matra
  • Espace III (1997-2002): Renault-designed, more refined
  • Espace IV (2002-2014): Avant-garde design, huge interior
  • Espace V (2015-present): SUV-styled, less van-like, still spacious

The Espace was ahead of its time. It offered seven seats, modular interior, and car-like driving. Every minivan today owes it a debt.

Alpine – The Revival

Alpine was a French sports car manufacturer, closely linked to Renault. Founded by Jean Rédélé in 1955, Alpine used Renault components in lightweight, sporty bodies.

Classic Alpine (1955-1995):

  • A106 (1955): First Alpine
  • A110 (1962-1977): The legend. Won the 1973 World Rally Championship. Beautiful, lightweight, and successful.
  • A310 (1971-1984): V6-powered, more GT
  • GTA (1984-1991): Turbo, stylish
  • A610 (1991-1995): V6 turbo, final classic

Modern Alpine (2017-present):

  • A110 (2017): Revival of the legend. Mid-engine, 1.8L turbo, 252 hp, 1,100 kg. Pure driving pleasure.
  • A110 S / GT / R: Performance variants

The new A110 is a masterpiece – light, agile, and beautiful. It proves that lightness still matters.

Renault Racing

Formula 1:

  • 1977-1985: First turbocharged F1 engine
  • 1992-1997: Engine supplier for Williams (championships)
  • 2002-2011: Renault works team – 2005-2006 championships with Fernando Alonso
  • 2016-present: Engine supplier for Red Bull (2010-2013 championships) and Alpine team

Rallying:

  • 1973: Alpine A110 wins first WRC championship
  • 1970s-80s: Renault 5 Turbo and Maxi Turbo
  • 1990s: Clio Williams in kit car form

Le Mans:

  • 1978: Alpine A442B wins Le Mans
  • 2013-2016: Signatech Alpine wins LMP2 class
